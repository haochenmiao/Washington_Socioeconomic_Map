Data Information Page

Sources:

All data used in this map was gathered from the Washington Geospatial Open Data Portal. In total, six separate datasets were gathered, with one key variable in the map being derived from each individual dataset. Shown in the table below are the datasets, the used variables, and the sources of the datasets.

Variable Description:

properties_Percent_Singleton_Live_Births

Number of live born singleton (one baby) infants born at term (at or above 37 completed weeks of gestation) with a birth weight of less than 2,500 grams (about 5.5 lbs.). (More information)

properties_Percent_People_of_Color

A sum of all race/ethnicity categories EXCEPT White/Non-Hispanic, this includes: Black, American Indian/Alaskan Native, Asian, Native Hawaiian-Other Pacific Islander, Two or more races and the ethnicity grouping of "Spanish/Hispanic/Latino". (More information)

properties_Percent_Without_Diploma

the percentage of people who have not received a high school diploma or GED by the age of 25. (More information)

properties_Percent_Living_in_Poverty

poverty from the ACS 5-year roll-up (2015- 2019), measured people living under 185% of the federal poverty level from Table S1701 columns for Population used to estimate poverty (HC01_EST_VC01), the number of people living below 185% (HC01_EST_VC55) and the associated margin of error (HC01_MOE_VC55). (More information)

properties_Percentage

the percent householders spend on housing costs for 2015-2019 (More information)

properties_Percent_Unemployed

the population of people 16 years and older that are in the labor force and registered as unemployed. This measure was derived from the following ACS Table "Employment Status" (DP03) columns HC02_VC05, HC01_VC07 for 2015 - 2019 (More information)

Browse Cities

A list of all cities in Washington state with coordinates allowing the users to 'fly to' or focus screens on selected cities (More information)

Treatment:

In order to allow for the data from each dataset to be readily comparable across different scales, each variable used in the map was normalized using min-max normalization. The normalized values of each variable were then used to get the height and color of census tract polygons. In the map, when a census tract is clicked upon the actual sselected value is shown after the census tract and is then followed by the census tract's normalized value.

Aside from min-max normalization, the geometry of the data was clipped using a cartographic boundary map from the United States census bureau. Clipping was performed on the data so as to limit census tract extents to only land area, thereby improving geographic legibility.
